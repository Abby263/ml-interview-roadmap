## The real goal of RAG questions

RAG questions are not just about retrieval mechanics. Interviewers use them to test whether you can design a grounded, measurable, and cost-aware AI product.

The strongest answers usually separate three layers:

- retrieval quality
- generation quality
- system operations

## Questions that come up repeatedly

### How do you choose chunk size?

Discuss document structure, answer granularity, embedding recall, and context budget. A weak answer says it depends and stops there. A stronger answer explains the trade-off between fragmenting context and diluting relevance.

### When do you add reranking?

Good answers explain that reranking helps when the first-pass retriever has acceptable recall but weak precision. It is usually a better next step than changing everything at once.

### How do you evaluate a RAG system?

Separate retrieval metrics from answer metrics. Then include faithfulness, citation accuracy, latency, and cost. If the workflow matters, add human review or task-completion metrics.

### When should you fine-tune instead of using RAG?

RAG and fine-tuning solve different problems. RAG is primarily about fresh or private knowledge. Fine-tuning is more about behavior, style, or repeated task adaptation.
