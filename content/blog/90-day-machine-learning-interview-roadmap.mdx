## What makes a 90-day roadmap useful

Most candidates already know the topics they could study. The real problem is sequencing. A good roadmap decides what to front-load, what to compress, and what needs repeated rehearsal.

For ML and AI interviews, the mistake is usually one of these:

- spending too long on familiar model theory and not enough on system design
- ignoring SQL and coding rounds because the role sounds research-heavy
- treating GenAI as prompt trivia instead of a systems topic
- waiting too long to practice speaking through trade-offs

## A practical cadence

Use the first month to rebuild confidence in coding, SQL, metrics, and core modeling. Use the second month to move through deep learning and GenAI. Use the final month to simulate the loop you expect to face.

That means:

1. foundations and classical ML first
2. transformers, RAG, and evaluation second
3. architecture, operations, and storytelling last

The order matters because system design answers become stronger when your evaluation and modeling language is already sharp.

## Weekly operating rhythm

Each week should include four different modes of practice:

- one concept refresh block
- one implementation or SQL block
- one interview-answer rehearsal block
- one weak-area review block

This reduces the common trap where candidates only read notes and then discover in the interview that they cannot explain anything cleanly.

## Final advice

A roadmap is useful only if it becomes a forcing function. Treat each phase as a deliverable, not a reading list. The goal is not to touch every topic. The goal is to become interview-safe across the highest-risk surfaces.
